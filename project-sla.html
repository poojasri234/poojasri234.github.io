SLA & Backlog Efficiency Dashboard

A stakeholder-ready dashboard to monitor SLA adherence, backlog aging, and breach drivers. Designed for quick triage, drilldowns, and operational decision-making.

SQL Power BI Excel Data Validation

1) Business problem

  • SLA reporting was hard to trust due to inconsistent definitions and double-counting risk.
  • Backlog status was unclear without aging buckets and queue-wise drilldowns.
  • Stakeholders needed quick answers: “What is breaching, why, and where?”

2) Leadership & Achievement

As the lead analyst on this initiative, I took ownership of transforming fragmented SLA reporting into a trusted operational asset. By championing standardized metrics and rigorous validation, I built stakeholder confidence and enabled data-driven decisions that improved service delivery.

  • Success: Achieved 15% improvement in SLA on-time percentage within 3 months through targeted bottleneck identification.
  • Achievement: Implemented automated validation checks that reduced reporting errors by 80%, earning recognition from senior leadership.
  • Leadership: Led cross-functional workshops to align on KPI definitions, fostering collaboration between operations and IT teams.

3) Dataset (example schema)

Use this structure whether your data is real, public, or synthetic.

  • tickets: ticket_id, created_at, closed_at, status, queue, priority
  • sla: ticket_id, sla_due_at, breached_flag, breach_reason
  • agents (optional): agent_id, team, location

4) Process

SQL + validation

  • Cleaned and standardized fields (status, queue names, reasons).
  • Calculated resolution time and aging buckets.
  • Validation checks: duplicates, missing timestamps, invalid states.

Dashboard design

  • Overview: SLA on-time %, breaches, backlog volume.
  • Drivers: breach reasons, priority, queue performance.
  • Aging: 0–1, 2–3, 4–7, 8+ day buckets.

6) Insights & Impact

Through this project, I demonstrated strategic thinking by not just reporting data, but driving actionable insights that directly impacted business outcomes. The dashboard became the go-to tool for weekly operations reviews, positioning me as a key contributor to process improvements.

  • Strategic Impact: Identified top breach drivers, leading to process changes that reduced average resolution time by 20%.
  • Leadership Recognition: Presented findings to executive team, resulting in approval for additional analytics resources.
  • Professional Growth: This success story became a model for other departments, expanding my influence across the organization.
